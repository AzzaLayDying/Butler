Note on Husband: Harold Robinson HICKTON (1)

Accidentally killed at Kai Iwi. He was managing a farm in Wanganui and was killed by a fall of earth. He had set dynamite which failed to go off. He went to investigate but it then exploded and caused a fall that buried him. There was a court case but little compensation. He is buried in Wanganui Cemetery in an unmarked grace. Block F, Row 5, PLot 346.

Note on Husband: Harold Robinson HICKTON (2)

He always hated the number 8 which waa connected with their baggage which was lefton the wharf at Auckland when they sailed to Russel after their marriage. They kept the hotel in Ohaiwai for a while and whilst there 2 daughters, Isabel and Elizabeth were born. Isabel was born in the corner room on the verandah. After leaving Ohaiwai they moved around a great deal. Harold is said to have worked in the gold fields for some tme. The Auckland directory of 1891 lists Alice as hotel keeper at the corner of Pollen & Mary Streets, Thames. Bertha was born in Waihi and remembers living at Puriri and Te Aroha. She remembers her mother being taken to hospital on a long dray and that they had a dog Rosie.

Note on Husband: Harold Robinson HICKTON (3)

Travellers Rest Family records suggest that for at least some of the time there, they lived in the ‘Travellers Rest’ a hotel and accommodation house at Ōhaeawai licenced to their son Harold Robinson Hickton in June 1883 . H. R Hickson had a daughter born in Waimate North in January 1889 so I suspect he continued to live there after the death of his father. I found reference to a Robinson Spencer leasing a hotel called the Travellers Rest in Te Ahuahu in 1892. This hotel overlooked the plains of Ohaeawai . It is likely that this is the same hotel. The hotel was a gathering place for the gumdiggers and the men of the great kauri logging camps. Over the muddy tracks and the summer dust, workmen came to quench their first at the Travellers Rest. Later that same year Robinson, purchased the hotel for 359pd. According to a Weekly News article, the Spencers owned the hotel until 1918 when it was sold by Henry Wyatt a nephew of Margaret Spencer's, who inherited the property after her death in 1918.

Note on Husband: Harold Robinson HICKTON (4)

Occupation

Harold managed the 'Traveler's Rest', and also worked as a clerk in a gum store. He and his wife Sofia travelled and change home a lot as the records of where their children were born show.

Note on Wife: Alice Sophia Hickton (nee MARSHALL)

Alice Sophia was born 1863 in a tent below the cliffs on the waterfront where the Farmers carpark stands today. (Note: according to Mrs Brownes story when the parents came to Auckland they lived in Williams Ln,so one version could be incorrect?). She went to a Cathloic school in Cook St (near where the Central Polica Station now stands). One Friday when her mother was sick the Father gave her meat hash to eat at lunch time. Someone told the Suisters and because of this she had ner nose rubbed in the dirt of the floor and was locked in a cupboard. She refused to go back to school and was later taught by a Mr Brabason who was a ventriliquist. She was poorly educated and because her husband was well educated there was a lot of friction in the marriage. She worked very hard but wa left to manage on her own for long periods while Harold was away working. They were living apart at the time of his death.
