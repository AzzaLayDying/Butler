Note on Wife: Betha (Twin) Beer (nee HICKTON)

Bertha remembers little of her father, saying she seldom saw him. She remembers one Xmas morning walking with him and her sister Eunice down Symond St. He was jangling money in his pocket. Bertha had always wanted a toy piano and he bought one for her and a toy dressing table for Eunice. Except for this once she does not remember having presents.

Bertha said Harold was a gambler so Alice always kept boarders but at one time they were turned out of their home by bailiffs who took all their possessions. Alica and the girls had to live on charity. Berha looked after Alice for 30 years till her mother died.
