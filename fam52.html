Note on Husband: Thomas Seon, CAPTAIN

Emigrated to NZ with Sophia on August 17th 1861 from Plymouth on sailing ship Black Eagle. Arrived in Christchurch NZ Nov 19th 1861.

With them were 3 children: Thomas, Henry & Laura. They settled in Auckland and 5 more children were born there, In 1871 they shifted up to Omapere near the Hokianga Harbor where in 1873 Thomas was appointed Harbor Master. Their 3 youngest sons Freddie, Ernest and Frank went to Omapere school and were still on the roll in 1875. By 1883 only ERnest and Frank were still there. There is no record of the older childrens school days, tho we do know that they all lived with their parents in Hokianga.

 

Marriage IGI ref: source 572510 ;batch m042891: refers to Sophia Smith or Murrell. Marriage certificate says Sophia was of Hackney. Bermuda Index Juniperhill Press, Toronto 1989 gives date of death as 15 Feb 1886. But the burial register for the Rawene cemetry says it was 16 February 1886. Capt Thomas Seon was the habourmaster from 13 Sept 1873 till his death. Would have lived in the harbourmasrter's house in Omapere. Passenger list of the Black Eagle (left Glasgow 10 Aug 1861 via London to Auckland (19 Nov 1861) includes: Thomas Seon Sophia Seon Henry Seon Laura Seon Ships & Masters to Sydney index includes SEON G/Thomas Agenoria 22/10/1851 San Francisco SEON Thos Akbar 27/12/1853 London Sydney Morning Herald 23 Aug 1852 The A1 Ship Agenoria Captain Thomas Seon ready to sail. Daily Southern Cross 22 Nov 1866 In Port Tasmanian Maid, p.s. Seon, from Wangarie Daily Southern Cross 19 Oct 1867 Port of Auckland Departures Enterprise No 2 p.s. 45 tons Seon for Kauwaeranga with passengers Advert in 1871 & Steamship Enterprise No 2 Capt Seon will sail for The Thames three days a week. Still on the same boat in 1873 Death register entry says that he died of Functional Liver Disease (24 days). Occupation Master Mariner Harbour Master &amp; Pilot in the service of the NZ government. 5 boys and two girls living.

Note on Wife: Sophia Seon (nee MURRELL)

Witnesses to first marriage included Emily Murrell ref for second marriage: IGI film source 572510; batch m042891 death cert says that she had been in NZ for 34 years ie went there in 1865. Listed in the 1900 PO Directory as of Symond Street, Auckland
