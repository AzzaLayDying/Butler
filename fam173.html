Note on Husband: John MCCURDY (1)

The name McCurdy was found on the passenger list of the sailing ship Queen Bee when it arrived in Auckland on 26th Oct 1872. It had left Gravesend England on 20th July 1882 and her final departure from Start Point on 24th July, a journey taking 97 days. There were 126 passengers on baird, 106 being Government immigrants, 16 were Irish. Listed were John McCurdy, wife Catrine and their 5 children.

*Mary 12 yrs, John 9 yrs, Rose 8 yrs, Daniel 4 yrsCatrine (Catherine) 2 yrs and Margaret 1 yr.

The cost of the fare was 71 pound 10 shillings (the currency at the time). Joqhn paid 20 pound leaving 51 pound 10 shillings to be paid by the governmenrt of the day. They went to live in Thames where only 2 yrs later Catrine died aged 40 yrs. Mary being the eldest brought up the family. in 1881-1887 John and John Jr were both lsited on the Electoral Roll as miners, we think that the name of their mine was "the Little Maggie". They were well known and respected by toher residents. John Snr left and came to Auckland where he ran a boarding house in Upper Queen St. He married again to Mary Ann O'Sullivan, a young girl, and took her back to Thames where they had 3 daughters. He died in Thames in 1903.
