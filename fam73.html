  • LifeSketch: OBITUARY. Mr. Arthur Brett dies after long illness, aged 83. Mr. Arthur Rowland Brett died at his home in Remuera early this morning after a long illness. He was 83 years of age. Eldest son of the late Sir Henry Brett, one of the founders of the Auckland Star, he was associated with the newspaper for most of his life. He was works manager of the paper for many years and also a director of the company until last year. Mr. Brett was born on the city side of the harbour before Sir Henry moved to Takapuna, but grew up at “Te Kiteroa,” now the Henry Brett Memorial Home. He was educated at Nelson College and at 17 entered the job printing department of the Star – in those days hand presses were used to produce the newspaper. As a young man he went to Sydney to work on the Evening News. In Australia he learnt the latest methods of newspaper production and was responsible for buying a rotary machine and presses which revolutionized the production of the Star. In later years Mr. Brett became works manager and held that position until his retirement in 1925. He was one of those responsible for obtaining modern equipment for the Star, including the first linotype machine in the Dominion. Even when he was works manager he was often to be found with his coat off in the stereotype department during rush hours. He became a director of the Brett Printing and Publishing Company on the death of Sir Henry Brett in 1927, and later was a director of New Zealand Newspapers, Ltd., the public company which replaced the Brett Company. He resigned in July last year owing to illness. An enthusiastic motorist, Mr. Brett owned one of the early cars in Auckland – a Buick with a crank handle at the side. In his earlier life he was a keen horseman, and bred many show winners at Milford, where he lived after his marriage. Mr. Brett was a foundation member of the New Zealand Power Boat Association founded in 1905, and now known as the Auckland Motor Yacht Club. He was commodore in 1909 and 1910, flying his flag from his 40ft motor cruiser Aileen, which he late replaced with the Norana. Auckland Star, 13 June 1949, Page 3.

Note on Wife: Eva Laura BRETT (nee BARKER)

Eva Laura Brett was educated at San Francisco and later lived with her family in Devonport. She was married to Arthur Brett for 46 yrs by the Archdeacon W. Calder. During her long residence in Devonport she took an active part in church work, and was esteemed for many unobtrusive deeds of kindness to those in need.
